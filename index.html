Handmade cloth dolls, stitched one at a time.

Scrappy Dolls is the studio of artist Kanda Kay — small, soft dolls hand-cut and hand-sewn from quilting cottons, vintage prints, and fabric remnants too lovely to throw away. No two are alike.

Made by hand. Made to keep.

Every Scrappy Doll begins as a pile of fabric — quilt offcuts, an old pillowcase, the last good piece of a favorite shirt. Kanda Kay cuts, pieces, and stitches each doll by hand, finishing with embroidered features and a name only that doll will ever wear.

The result is a small, sturdy companion: warm-feeling, washable, and unmistakably one of a kind.

What makes a Scrappy Doll different

One-of-a-kind

Every doll is a unique combination of fabrics, faces, and finishings. You'll never see another exactly like yours.

Made from fabric with a story

Vintage prints, quilt remnants, and rescued scraps — each doll carries the texture and history of the cloth it came from.

Hand-stitched, built to last

Cut, sewn, stuffed, and finished by hand. Sturdy seams and embroidered features mean these dolls hold up to real love.

Low waste, high heart

Sewing scrappy gives beautiful fabric a second life — the kind of object that gets prettier the longer you have it.

Frequently asked questions

Are dolls available to purchase?

New dolls are announced on the Scrappy Dolls Facebook page as they're finished. Send a message there to ask about what's currently available.

Can a doll be made from my own fabric?

Memory dolls — made from a child's outgrown clothing, a wedding dress, a beloved quilt — are part of what scrappy dolls are best at. Reach out via Facebook to talk through your fabric and what you'd like.

Are Scrappy Dolls safe for children?

Dolls are sewn with sturdy seams and embroidered features (no buttons or small attached parts), and stuffed with fiberfill. They make wonderful keepsakes and play companions for older babies and children.

How do I care for a Scrappy Doll?

Spot clean with a damp cloth and mild soap. For a deeper clean, hand-wash gently in cool water and reshape while damp; lay flat to dry.
